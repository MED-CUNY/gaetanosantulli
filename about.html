About the Laboratory

Integrating basic science, translational research, and clinical insight

Mission

The mission of the laboratory is to advance the understanding of cardiovascular and metabolic diseases through rigorous mechanistic research and clinically relevant translational approaches. By integrating molecular biology, physiology, and patient-oriented investigation, the lab aims to identify novel pathways and therapeutic targets that improve human health.

Research Philosophy

Our research philosophy is grounded in scientific rigor, innovation, and reproducibility. We prioritize hypothesis-driven investigation while embracing emerging technologies and interdisciplinary collaboration. Particular emphasis is placed on mechanisms that bridge cellular dysfunction with organ-level disease and clinical outcomes.

Translational Focus

A defining feature of the laboratory is its strong translational orientation. Discoveries at the bench are pursued with a clear vision toward clinical relevance, informing biomarker development, therapeutic strategies, and patient stratification. Clinical observations, in turn, help guide experimental priorities and model development.

Training and Mentorship

The laboratory is committed to the training and mentorship of students, fellows, and early-career investigators. Trainees are encouraged to develop independence, critical thinking skills, and a strong ethical foundation. Alumni of the lab pursue careers in academia, clinical medicine, industry, and public health.
