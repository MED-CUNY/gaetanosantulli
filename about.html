Biography

Prof. Gaetano Santulli is a Full Professor of Medicine (Cardiology) and Principal Investigator of the Santulli Lab. He has dedicated his career to the study of cardiovascular and metabolic diseases, with particular emphasis on translational research and the integration of molecular biology into clinical practice.

Research Interests

His main research interests include the molecular mechanisms of heart failure, arrhythmias, diabetes-induced cardiovascular complications, and aging-related cardiac dysfunction. His laboratory applies a combination of in vitro, in vivo, and clinical studies to uncover novel therapeutic targets.

Education & Training

Prof. Santulli received his MD and PhD degrees, followed by extensive postdoctoral training in cardiovascular research. He has also completed specialized clinical training in cardiology and geriatric medicine.

Professional Achievements

He has published extensively in high-impact journals, has an H-index >100, and has been recognized internationally with numerous awards and honors. He serves on editorial boards, study sections, and as a reviewer for top cardiovascular and biomedical journals.

Key Collaborations

Prof. Santulli collaborates with leading research centers worldwide, focusing on innovative projects that bridge basic science and clinical applications. His team includes postdoctoral fellows, research scientists, and graduate students actively engaged in cutting-edge cardiovascular research.
